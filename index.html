Summary of Boston Harbor Cleanup:

The harbor at Boston was considered one of the dirtiest harbors by the 1980s. The large volume of sewage and wastewater that was being dumped into the harbor made it very polluted and unpleasant to the point that businesses and people avoided the area. After the Clean Water Act was passed, responsibility to clean up the water was passed onto the Massachusetts Water Resource Authority. About $3.8 billion was spent on this project. As a result, it led to a boom in economic activities from the growth of businesses, residential spaces, and recreational areas at the water front. It is now known as one of Americaâ€™s greatest environmental achievements. Discussed in the article, a study used to understand the economic impact of the Boston Harbor Cleanup concluded that the benefits and values received exceeds the costs of the cleanup.
